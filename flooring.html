Hardwood vs Laminate.

Homeowners always find themselves contemplating between choosing to go with the cheaper laminate flooring or to go with a more solid and long term solution such as installing hardwood. Today we have additional options such as engineered hardwood, vinyl flooring and even fibreglass. It's important to understand the pros and cons of each type of installation and how you can benefit.

Laminate

Laminate installation is the cheaper, simpler alternative to re-flooring your home. Installing laminate can be turn out to be a quick job especially with some of the new click technology available with an increasing number of laminate manufacturing companies. Laminate is able to handle the test of time but doens't the rich wooden look that hard wood has. It is though a very budget friendly approach.

Hardwood

Hardwood floors create a warm and inviting look. The natural feel of some of the hand-scraped hardwood floors is simply a joy to walk on barefoot. Hardwood though more costly, is a great option when deciding to remodel long-term. Hardwood floors come in a range of colours and widths as well as textures and finishes. Hardwood can be installed in different patterns and angles complementing any room.

Engineered

An engineered wood floor is constructed of layers of both hardwood and plywood, where the top layer is real wood and the bottom layers are there for durability. Engineered wood is said to be able to handle slightly lower and higher humidity. The top layer can also be sanded once and slightly the second to be refinished. It looks great and can be installed in a few more places than hardwood.
